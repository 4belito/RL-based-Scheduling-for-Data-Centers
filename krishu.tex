\begin{frame}{Context: Why This Work Exists}
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \column{0.56\textwidth}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Data centers and HPC clusters process mixed workloads: short interactive jobs, long training jobs, and priority tasks.
        \item The scheduler must decide \textit{which job runs where and when} under CPU/GPU/memory constraints.
        \item Poor decisions increase queue delay, waste capacity, and raise power/cooling cost.
        \item BU need: an adaptive scheduler that is both effective and easy to explain to non-ML stakeholders.
      \end{itemize}

      \vspace{0.3em}
      \begin{block}{Core objective}
        Learn a policy that improves latency, utilization, and energy together under real variability.
      \end{block}

\begin{frame}{Background 1/2: Scheduling Landscape}
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \column{0.56\textwidth}
      \small
      \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{What the scheduler observes:} queue state (arrival time, size, deadline) and machine state (free CPU/GPU/memory).
        \item \textbf{What makes it hard:} arrivals are stochastic, jobs have different resource profiles, and constraints change over time.
        \item \textbf{Why heuristics struggle:} FIFO is fair but can increase tail latency; SJF reduces average time but may starve long jobs.
        \item \textbf{Operational consequence:} local decisions can cause global fragmentation and lower throughput.
      \end{itemize}

      \vspace{0.3em}
      \begin{block}{Key takeaway}
        Scheduling is a constrained, dynamic control problem rather than a static sorting problem.
      \end{block}

    \column{0.4\textwidth}

\begin{frame}{Background 2/2: Why RL Fits}
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \column{0.56\textwidth}
      \small
      \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Sequential nature:} each placement changes future queueing, resource availability, and downstream decisions.
        \item \textbf{Delayed feedback:} the quality of a decision is visible later through job completion, SLA violation, and energy trends.
        \item \textbf{RL advantage:} optimize long-term return instead of a one-step rule.
        \item \textbf{Business control:} reward design directly maps policy behavior to BU priorities.
      \end{itemize}

      \vspace{0.2em}
      \[
        \text{Return} = \sum_{t} \eta^t r_t
      \]
      \vspace{-0.3em}
      {\footnotesize This is exactly why RL is suitable: it optimizes cumulative impact, not instantaneous gain.}
